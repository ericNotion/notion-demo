Problem 2
People don't know what to automate.

Before you can build a workflow, you need to understand the problem.

Where do things get stuck? What takes the most time? What does a good outcome look like?

Most workflow tools skip this step entirely. They hand you a blank canvas and say "go."

Questions you need to answer first
?

Which steps take the longest, cause the most rework
?

Time per task, people involved, error rates
?

The ideal output at each stage, not just the stages themselves
?
Where should AI vs. humans be?
Which decisions need human judgment, which are rote
3
Our Approach
Two principles.
🔍
Be a consultant
Don't just hand people a blank canvas. Help them understand the problem first — the bottlenecks, the costs, the opportunities for AI.
📝
Work with concrete examples
Don't make people write abstract specs. Show them a real task flowing through the system. The example is the spec.
4
The Method
Design the output.
AI derives the rules.

This is program synthesis from examples.

You craft an ideal trace for a concrete task. The system generalizes it into prompts and checks that work for any task.

Same idea as evals-driven development: specify the test, not the implementation.

Step 4
React to the output.
Don't edit prompts.

Each stage produces output. You give it a thumbs up or thumbs down.

Thumbs up pins it as the "gold master" — the ideal result to aim for.

Thumbs down? Just say what's wrong. AI adjusts the instructions for you.

Step 5
Run the agent.
Compare to the ideal.

Execute the real AI agent on the same task.

The system puts the actual output next to your gold master, and an LLM-as-judge scores the gap.

Iterate on settings until the real output converges with what you designed.
